# printer.cfg — required edits

Only ONE change is needed: add two `[include]` lines near the top of your
existing `printer.cfg`.

## Find this line (it's at the very top of your printer.cfg):

    [include fluidd.cfg]

## Insert these two lines immediately after it:

    [include heatsoak.cfg]
    [include print_macros.cfg]

## Result (first few lines of printer.cfg should look like this):

    [include fluidd.cfg]
    [include heatsoak.cfg]
    [include print_macros.cfg]

    [exclude_object]

    [extruder_config_bak]

    [print_task_config]

    [mcu]
    serial: /dev/ttyS6
    ...

That's it. Save printer.cfg and Klipper will auto-restart with the new
macros loaded.

## Why these two specifically must come AFTER fluidd.cfg:

- fluidd.cfg defines the base PAUSE / RESUME / CANCEL_PRINT macros.
- heatsoak.cfg overrides RESUME using `rename_existing` — this only works
  if RESUME exists when heatsoak.cfg is parsed.
- print_macros.cfg's PRINT_WARMUP references HEAT_SOAK from heatsoak.cfg,
  so heatsoak.cfg must come first.

## What NOT to change:

- Do NOT modify the [heater_bed] block. max_temp=100 is correct for U1.
- Do NOT modify [idle_timeout]. timeout_on_pause: 9999999999 already
  handles the bed-shutoff problem during soaks.
- Do NOT touch [temperature_sensor cavity] or [fan_generic cavity_fan].
  They're already correct and the macros reference them by name.
- Do NOT touch anything below the `#*# <-------- SAVE_CONFIG -------->`
  marker — that section is auto-generated by Klipper.
